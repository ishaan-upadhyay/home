Basic information

This era was a byproduct of agrarian communities exploding in the Vedic Period. Referred to as the 2nd urbanization due to the break in city-building post the demise of the Indus Valley Civilization, this was the period that shaped India for the years to come.

Uniform urbanization

The second urbanization is so-called as it is the first set of large scale civilizations after the Indus Valley's fall. This flowering occurred in the Gangetic Plain, another area flush with fertile soil. Due to advanced agricultural techniques and better practices, surplus grain was produced. Business communities began to form, and trade became more widespread with the use of the coin.

Mahajanapadas

The Mahajanapadas were a collection of monarchies and republics, 16 in number, spread across the Gangetic plains. These were the most massive at the time in India, The names of thse are Kashi, Kosala, Magadha, Anga, Vriji/Vaiji, Malla, Vatsa, Kuru, Panchale, Mastsy, Shurasena, Assaka, Avanti, Gandhara, and Kamboja.

Mahajanapada: Magadha

The Mahajanapada of Magadha was responsible for some of the greatest empires to ever exist throughout India. This place had 2 capitals, Rajagriha, presently Rajgir, and Pataliputra, presently Patna.

Mauryan Empire

The Mauryan empire was the first empire to completely encompass India (save for South India), and also one of the largest to ever exist. It was established by Chandragupta Maurya, the empire's namesake, assisted by a man known as Chanakya. The greatest known emperor of this dynasty was Ashoka, under whose rule the empire reached its greatest extent. He was known for his embracement of Buddhism following his war with the Kalingans, which spread Buddhism throughout Asia.

Sangam Period

South India has historically been independent of other empires, so between 300 BCE and 400 CE Tamil literature became very expansive, written by several people. These books have a variety of subject matter contained within them, ranging from history to culture.
